I am Professor of Data Science and Head of the Department of Statistics at the London School of Economics. My research is in machine learning, with an emphasis on the design and analysis of algorithms for sequential decision-making, learning under uncertainty, and optimisation.

Much of this work is motivated by questions that arise in AI systems: how to learn reliably from structured or limited feedback, how to align learning objectives with human preference, and how to scale optimisation to large models. Earlier contributions to gradient compression for distributed training (QSGD, NeurIPS 2017) and to ranking and inference from pairwise comparisons connect directly to the challenges of training and aligning modern language models. Current work examines alignment objectives in post-training (GRPO), bandit and active learning algorithms, and scalable calibration methods deployed at web scale.

I am a Visiting Scientist at Meta (Central Applied Science) and a Faculty Affiliate of the LSE Data Science Institute. I am happy to discuss potential collaborations.
