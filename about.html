About

Milan Vojnovic is Professor of Data Science and Head of the Department of Statistics at the London School of Economics. His research is in machine learning, with a focus on the design and analysis of algorithms for sequential decision-making, learning under uncertainty, and optimisation. He is particularly interested in the theoretical foundations of modern AI systems, including reinforcement learning, alignment, and scalable training methods.

He is a Visiting Scientist at Meta (Central Applied Science), a position he has held in various capacities since 2019, and a Faculty Affiliate of the LSE Data Science Institute. Prior to joining LSE in 2016, he spent twelve years as a researcher at Microsoft Research in Cambridge, working across machine learning, distributed systems, and network optimisation. He also held an appointment as Affiliated Lecturer at the Statistical Laboratory, University of Cambridge.

He received his PhD in Technical Sciences from the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in 2003, and his MSc and BSc in Electrical Engineering from the University of Split, Croatia. He is the author of Contest Theory: Incentive Mechanisms and Ranking Methods (Cambridge University Press, 2016).

His work has been recognised with the ACM SIGMETRICS Rising Star Researcher Award, the ERCIM Cor Baayen Award, best paper awards at IEEE INFOCOM, ACM SIGMETRICS, and IEEE IWQoS, and research awards from Facebook and Criteo. Research from his group has been deployed in production systems at Meta, Facebook, Microsoft Bing, and Microsoft Live.
